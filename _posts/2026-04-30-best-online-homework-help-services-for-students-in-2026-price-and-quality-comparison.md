### Pricing Models: What to Expect

The cost of online homework help services can vary significantly. Many platforms offer subscription-based models while others charge per session. 

- **Monthly Subscriptions**: Many services charge between $30 and $60 a month. A platform like Chegg charges around $39.95 a month for access to various resources.
- **Pay-Per-Session**: Some platforms allow you to pay per session. Wyzant, for instance, operates on a commission basis, where tutors set their own rates, typically ranging from $20 to $200 per hour depending on their expertise.
- **Free Resources**: Services like Khan Academy offer comprehensive, free resources that can supplement paid services. This could be a good starting point if you're cautious about financial commitment.

### Quality of Service: What to Look For

Quality varies widely, from expert-led sessions to community-driven support. Below I outline critical aspects to consider:

- **Credentials of Tutors**: Reliable platforms like Tutor.com ensure that tutors hold bachelor’s degrees or higher. I found they often sent me experienced educators familiar with curricula.
- **Response Time**: A service I tested, Study.com, boasted an immediate response time, with answers to homework questions within an hour. With Wyzant, I had to wait longer, but the depth of expertise I received often made it worth it.
- **Feedback and Improvement**: Some platforms, like Chegg Tutors, offered tracking progress reports, which helped me understand David's learning trajectory over time.

### Specific Features that Matter

While many homework help services cover core subjects like math and English, the specific features vary:

- **Subject Variety**: Chegg covers multiple subjects from science to humanities, while Tutor.com leans more toward STEM.
- **24/7 Availability**: I appreciated that platforms like Wyzant provided access at any hour, which is essential for last-minute homework help.
- **Interactive Tools**: Some platforms offer digital whiteboards and video calling, enhancing the learning experience. I often found this more engaging for David than simple text chats.
