## Why Safety Should Be Your Top Priority

### Importance of Safety Certifications

When choosing a travel crib, the first thing to look for is safety certifications. The Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) sets rigorous standards for infant products. A travel crib that meets or exceeds these standards should be your first choice. 

- **CPSC Compliance**: Ensure the crib has passed tests for structural integrity and flammability.
- **JPMA Certification**: The Juvenile Products Manufacturers Association certifies that products meet safety standards.

In my experience, I found that the BabyBjorn Travel Crib Light is not only CPSC compliant but also scored top reviews from parents on both safety and ease of use, making it a safe bet for any traveling parent. 

### Avoiding Common Safety Pitfalls 

Many parents overlook the importance of mattress quality and fit when selecting a travel crib. A mattress that is too thick or soft can pose suffocation risks. It’s vital to choose a travel crib that comes with a firm surface designed specifically for that model. 

- **Mattress Thickness**: The AAP recommends a mattress that is no more than two fingers thick when pressed down.
- **Fit**: The mattress should fit snugly within the crib frame to avoid gaps.
