## What Most Guides Get Wrong

Despite many guides available, there are common misconceptions when it comes to selecting camping gear.

1. **Brand Loyalty Over Value**: Not every high-end brand means quality. I once spent $200 on a “premium” tent that performed poorly in the rain, while a $100 tent from Coleman kept us dry.
   
2. **Overpacking Isn’t Always Better**: Guides often suggest bringing every possible item. In my experience, choosing multi-functional gear is much more effective and significantly lightens your load.
   
3. **Price Doesn’t Dictate Option Suitability**: Just because something is overpriced doesn’t guarantee better performance. I learned this when I bought a $150 camping stove only to find a better-performing one for $80 online.
