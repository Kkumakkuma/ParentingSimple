Different airlines have varying rules regarding car seats. For instance, while Delta allows car seats for children under 2 years, some budget airlines may require you to check them. Always check ahead to avoid surprises.

The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) recommends that children under 40 pounds be secured in a child restraint system approved for use in aircraft. This means that both [Brand A] and [Brand B] are acceptable for air travel, allowing parents peace of mind.

### How long should my child stay in a car seat?

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that children remain in a rear-facing car seat until they reach the highest weight or height allowed by the car seat's manufacturer. For most kids, this typically means until they're at least 2 years old, or even longer.

### Can I use a car seat on public transportation? 

Yes, public transport options generally allow car seats, but check local regulations. I found it easier to use a portable option like [Brand C] while navigating city buses with my kids.

### How do I know my car seat is installed correctly? 

You can check it by using the "Pinch Test" to see if the harness is fitted correctly. If you can pinch any excess webbing at the shoulders, the harness is too loose.
